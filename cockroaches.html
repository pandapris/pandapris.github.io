Cockroaches

Introduction

Cockroach is an insect from the Blattodea order. There are about 4000 species of cockroaches but only a handful of species are considered pests. In Malaysia, there are two species of cockroaches considered to be a home-based commercial pest, American cockroach (Periplaneta americana) and German cockroach (Blattella germanica).

Reasons Cockroach considered as pests

  1. Contaminate food with abandoned stools, dead bodies, skinbuilding, etc.
  2. Give an unpleasant odor.
  3. Generate allergy to sensitive people and can trigger asthma.
  4. Bring an illness to a human being where it body can transmit germs to human food
  5. Entomophobia - Feeling disturbed and fearful of cockroaches.

Areas cockroaches are found

  1. Sewers and drains.
  2. Food storage and waste disposal site
  3. Hidden area (furniture, wardrobe, drawer, and cabinet)
  4. Basement

Treatments

  1. Sanitation - Reduce water and food sources by cleaning food and waste debris and repair leak water pipes.
  2. Chemical Method:
    • Residual insecticides - Cockroaches will be exposed to residual insecticides when they travel on treated surfaces.
    • Gel traps - Placing insecticides in limited quantities in areas where cockroaches will go out to find food.
    • Dust insecticides- Used in places that cannot be in contact with water because it can cause toxic effects and which may cause damage to electrical appliances.
