Mosquitoes

Introduction

Mosquitoes are pesticides that bites human to consume blood and can transmit disease. Mosquitoes are insects that can be found anywhere in the forest, city, indoors, livestock farms etc. There are about 3450 species and subspecies of mosquitoes that all belong to 38 genus. All belong to the Culicidae family, divided into three subfamilies: Toxorhynchitinae, Anophelinae, and Culcinae. Toxorhynchites also called elephant mosquito or mosquito eater, is a genus of diurnal and often relatively colorful mosquitoes Anopheles contains the mosquitoes that transmit malarial parasites to humans and other animals. Some species of Anopheles also transmit microfilariae and arboviruses. Culcinae include the Aedes sp. That can transmit dengue virus. Female mosquitoes suck our blood. Male mosquitoes feed on plant nectars. They can develop from egg to adult in 10 to 14 days. They are most active from dusk to dawn and will fly up to 14 miles for a blood meal.

Reasons mosquitoes are considered as pests

  1. Become a vector of diseases such as Dengue, Malaria, and Filariasis.
  2. Bite and sucks human blood and animals.

Areas mosquitoes are found

  1. In the reservoir areas that become the breeding ground.
  2. Brackish coastal water to freshwater inland areas, especially mangroves, paddy fields, trenches, puddles, and ponds.
  3. Stagnant water

Treatments

  1. Reducing resources of habitat and environmental management (Clean the area; get rids of stagnant water)
  2. Biological control - Using Bacillus thuringiensis and Bacillus sphaericus which contains spores that produce toxins that specifically target and only affect the larvae of the mosquito. Introduce mosquito-eating fish such as gambusia, green sunfish, bluegills and minnows to standing water
  3. Chemical Control- The use of insecticides and Insect Growth Regulators (IGR which can be divided into juvenile hormones and chitin synthetic inhibitors)
  4. Physical attachment and self-protection - mosquito nets treated with insecticides, insecticides (aerosols, electric mats, and liquid evaporators), mosquito repellents and body insect repellents.
  5. Integrated Pest Management.
